# New Opportunity Positioning

Framework for positioning any offer as a new category rather than an improved version of an existing one. Category creation converts. Improvement claims commoditize.

## Setup

Check `.agents/product-marketing-context.md` first. Use what exists. Ask only for what's missing:
- What is the product or service?
- Who is the dream customer (specific identity)?
- What have they tried before that hasn't worked?
- What does this approach do differently at a mechanism level?

---

## The Core Distinction

There are two ways to position any offer:

**Improvement Offer:** "Do what you're already doing, but better, faster, or cheaper."

**New Opportunity:** "Here is a completely different vehicle that makes your old approach obsolete."

**Why improvement offers underperform:**
When you tell someone their current approach can be improved, you are implicitly reminding them of prior failures and asking them to admit they have been wrong. The cognitive defense activates. They say "I'll think about it."

When you present a new opportunity, you give them a fresh start with no baggage. The old vehicle failed, not them. Now there is something new that makes everything before it irrelevant.

**The test:** Could a competitor put their logo on your positioning and it would still be accurate? If yes, you are in improvement territory. New opportunity positioning is inherently non-interchangeable.

---

## The Three Components of a New Opportunity

### 1. The New Vehicle (Named)
A distinct mechanism, method, system, or category with a proper name. The name creates the category. Without a name, there is no category.

**Naming principles:**
- 2–4 words (noun-based)
- Describes the mechanism or transformation, not the features
- Memorable and ownable — would you be able to say "I use [name]" in a sentence?
- Avoids generic marketing words (innovative, revolutionary, proven, advanced)

Good vehicle names: "Sales Funnel System," "Metabolic Reset Protocol," "Revenue Intelligence OS," "Productized Service Model," "Microbiome-First Skincare System"

Bad vehicle names: "Comprehensive Marketing Solution," "Advanced Growth Platform," "Proven Results Program"

### 2. The Mechanism Story (Why It Works)
A simplified explanation of why the new vehicle produces results that the old vehicle cannot. This is not technical documentation. It is an emotionally accessible story about causality.

Structure:
> "The reason [old approach] fails is because it targets [wrong mechanism]. What we discovered is that [desired result] actually depends on [right mechanism]. And [vehicle name] is the first [category] designed specifically around [right mechanism]."

The mechanism story makes the new opportunity feel logical and necessary, not just novel.

### 3. Category Proof
Evidence that the new category is real and producing results. This goes beyond testimonials to include:
- Named clients or case studies with before/after specificity
- Market adoption signals (notable companies, publications, practitioners using it)
- Your own results as the first practitioner

## The Positioning Process: Step by Step

**Step 1: Map what they've tried**
List every vehicle the dream customer has used to try to get the desired result. Be comprehensive. Include things that worked partially and things that failed entirely.

**Step 2: Identify the structural flaw in each prior vehicle**
Why did each one fail? Not because the customer lacked willpower or execution — but because the vehicle itself had a mechanism-level flaw that made the result they wanted structurally impossible.

This is the crucial reframe. The customer has not failed. The vehicle failed them.

**Step 3: Name the alternative mechanism**
What does your approach target at a mechanism level that prior approaches missed? This needs to be genuinely different, not just described differently.

**Step 4: Name the vehicle**
Give the new mechanism a name. This is a marketing act. The name creates the category.

**Step 5: Build the before/after contrast**
What does life look like before the new vehicle (using the old approach)? What does it look like after (using the new vehicle)?

The contrast must be emotionally specific, not just functionally different.

**Step 6: Confirm the new opportunity test**
- Could a competitor use your exact positioning and have it be accurate? (If yes, go deeper)
- Does believing in your vehicle automatically dissolve the main objections? (If no, re-examine the mechanism)
- Can you describe the old vehicle's flaw in a way your dream customer immediately recognizes from their own experience? (If not, you haven't found the right mechanism story yet)

## Opportunity Framing Examples by Context

### DTC Physical Products

| Old Framing | New Opportunity Framing |
|-------------|------------------------|
| "Better protein powder" | "The first protein formula designed around female hormonal cycles" |
| "Faster-acting sleep aid" | "The circadian reset protocol that repairs your sleep architecture rather than sedating you" |
| "Premium skincare" | "The microbiome-first system that treats skin as an ecosystem, not a surface" |
| "Effective fat burner" | "The cellular energy protocol that restores mitochondrial function rather than relying on stimulants" |

In DTC physical, the product becomes the physical delivery mechanism of the new framework. You are not selling a capsule. You are selling a system, and the capsule is the key component.

### DTC Info Products

| Old Framing | New Opportunity Framing |
|-------------|------------------------|
| "Better marketing training" | "The Funnel-Based Growth System: the direct-response framework that replaces content marketing" |
| "Business coaching" | "The Productized Service Model: how service businesses add revenue without adding headcount" |
| "Social media course" | "Micro-Content Architecture: the system that generates 30 days of content in 2 focused hours" |
| "Email marketing training" | "The Relationship Revenue Framework: why your list size doesn't matter and what does" |

### B2B AI Tooling / Consulting

| Old Framing | New Opportunity Framing |
|-------------|------------------------|
| "AI-powered CRM" | "Revenue Intelligence: the system that knows which deals are real before your reps do" |
| "Automation consulting" | "The Human-in-the-Loop Operating System: AI that amplifies your team rather than replacing them" |
| "AI chatbot for support" | "The Zero-Wait Customer Experience: autonomous resolution for 70% of tickets without a human ever seeing them" |
| "Process automation" | "Workflow Intelligence: the AI-native ops layer that eliminates the work between your tools" |

In B2B, new opportunity framing must also address organizational fear. Frame the risk of staying with the old vehicle as greater than the risk of adopting the new one.

## Positioning Audit: Red Flags

Run existing copy or positioning through this. Any yes = needs reframing.

- [ ] Does the headline contain "better," "faster," "easier," "more," "proven," or "advanced"?
- [ ] Does the copy compare features or specs to competitors?
- [ ] Could a competitor swap their logo onto your positioning and have it still be accurate?
- [ ] Does the value proposition lead with what the product *is* rather than what the customer *experiences*?
- [ ] Do testimonials describe incremental gains rather than a fundamental change in approach?
- [ ] Is the mechanism explanation more than 2 sentences of technical language without a "kinda like" bridge?

## Naming Your Vehicle: Brainstorm Framework

Work through each dimension and look for combinations:

**Mechanism word** (what it targets or uses):
Cellular, Metabolic, Funnel-Based, Revenue, Circadian, Microbiome, Behavioral, Identity, Compounding, Systems...

**Transformation word** (what it produces or changes):
Reset, Intelligence, Architecture, Framework, Model, Protocol, System, Method, Map, Engine, OS...

**Domain word** (optional — adds specificity):
Growth, Revenue, Energy, Clarity, Sleep, Skin, Acquisition, Retention, Creative, Pipeline...

Combine: "[Mechanism] [Transformation]" or "[Domain] [Mechanism] [Transformation]"

Examples:
- Cellular + Energy + Protocol = **Cellular Energy Protocol**
- Revenue + Intelligence + OS = **Revenue Intelligence OS**
- Funnel + Based + Growth + System = **Funnel-Based Growth System**
- Microbiome + First + System = **Microbiome-First System**

Test the name: "I use the [name] to [what it does]." Does that sentence feel real and ownable? If yes, it is a strong candidate.
