# Funnel Copywriting

Expert Secrets story-based selling frameworks for funnel copy, VSLs, webinar scripts, and launch pages. Builds belief through narrative rather than features and benefits.

## Setup

**Always read product context first.**
Check `.agents/product-marketing-context.md` (or `.claude/product-marketing-context.md`). Use it. Only ask for what's missing.

**Minimum inputs needed before writing:**
- Who is the dream customer (specific role/identity, not just "entrepreneurs")
- What is the desired result (emotionally specific, not just functional)
- What is the new opportunity / vehicle name
- What false beliefs does this customer carry about the category, about themselves, and about their circumstances
- What is the origin story (yours or a key customer's)

If any of these are missing, ask before writing.

---

## Core Principle

> "Your goal is not to sell anyone anything. Your goal is to guide them to their own decision."

Funnel copy built on this principle uses stories to recreate the emotional experience that created belief in the expert — so the reader arrives at the same belief themselves and sells themselves.

**Logic doesn't sell. Emotion sells. Logic justifies.**

People buy based on an emotional epiphany. Then they use features, data, and guarantees to justify the decision they already made. Write for the emotional epiphany first. Features and logic come after.

---

## The Big Domino: Start Here

Before writing a single word, identify the ONE belief that, if held by the prospect, makes every other required belief fall automatically.

**Formula:**
> "If [specific customer] believes that [named vehicle/method] is the key to [emotionally specific desired result], then they will buy."

**Test:** Does believing this one thing dissolve all major objections automatically? If not, you haven't found the real Big Domino yet.

**The entire funnel — headline, hook, stories, offer, closes — exists to knock down this one domino.** Every element that doesn't serve the Big Domino should be cut or reworked.

### Big Domino by Context

**DTC Physical:** "If [customer] believes [mechanism name] is the actual biological/physiological reason [result] is possible — and [product] is the access point — they buy without hesitation."

**DTC Info Product:** "If [customer] believes [named system] is the most direct path to [identity shift + outcome] — and it's accessible to someone like them — they pay any reasonable price."

**B2B AI/Consulting:** "If [ICP role + company type] believes [approach] separates winners from laggards in their category — and [company] is the lowest-risk path to it — they book a call."

## Positioning: New Opportunity vs. Improvement Offer

**This is the most important positioning decision in the entire funnel.**

| Improvement Offer (Avoid) | New Opportunity (Use) |
|--------------------------|----------------------|
| "Better, faster, easier X" | "A completely different vehicle to [result]" |
| Reminds them of prior failures | Removes the shame of prior failures |
| Creates resistance | Creates curiosity |
| Triggers "I've tried this" | Triggers "I've never seen this before" |

**The improvement framing kills conversion because it asks the customer to admit they've been wrong.** The new opportunity framing gives them a fresh start — the old vehicle failed, not them.

**Naming the vehicle is mandatory.** An unnamed method feels vague. A named, diagrammable framework feels like proprietary IP and a distinct category worth investing in.

Red flags your copy is trapped in improvement framing:
- Headline contains "better," "faster," "easier," "more proven"
- Copy compares features to competitors
- Offer description could apply to any competitor

## The Four Core Stories

Every complete funnel must address three false belief categories. Map them before writing.

**The three false belief categories:**
1. **Vehicle** — "Does this method/product actually work?"
2. **Internal** — "Can I personally do this? Am I capable?"
3. **External** — "Will my specific circumstances prevent success?"

**The four story types that address them:**

### Story 1: Origin / Epiphany Bridge (Vehicle Belief)
Your personal story of discovering the new opportunity. Use the Epiphany Bridge structure (see references/epiphany-bridge-script.md). This story goes in the hook/intro of any funnel.

### Story 2: Vehicle Framework Story (Vehicle Belief — reinforced)
A story about how a specific component of your framework works. Teaches the *what* (strategy), never the *how* (tactics). The desire for the *how* is why they buy.

### Story 3: Internal Belief Story (Identity Belief)
Addresses "I'm not capable/qualified/disciplined enough." Must feature someone even less inherently qualified than your prospect who succeeded using the vehicle. The "they did it, so can I" transfer.

### Story 4: External Belief Story (Circumstance Belief)
Addresses specific circumstances the prospect uses as reasons they can't succeed. Must be specific: not "anyone can do it" but "here's someone who had your exact constraint and what happened."

**Audit:** if your funnel is missing any of the three false belief categories, that's where your conversion is leaking.

## The Epiphany Bridge Script Structure

Use this for your origin story and for each framework story in the Three Secrets.

```
[BACKSTORY — SET UP DESIRE AND CONFLICT]
Before I discovered [new opportunity], I was [specific relatable struggle].
I had tried [previous attempts]. The problem wasn't effort — it was the vehicle.
I remember [specific scene: place, time, sensory details].
I felt [specific emotion]. [Physical sensation description.]
What I wanted more than anything was [desire matching customer desire].
But [specific obstacle — mirrors customer's obstacle].

[THE EPIPHANY — THE AHA WITH THE GUIDE]
Then [guide appeared — person, book, conversation, moment].
[Guide] said/showed something I had never heard before: [the new idea, in simple language].
I remember feeling [emotion — curiosity, disbelief, excitement].
Something shifted. For the first time, I realized [the new belief].

[THE JOURNEY + RESULT]
So I [first action taken based on new belief].
At first, [obstacle or setback — builds credibility].
But I kept [what you continued].
Then [the specific result — include timing and magnitude].

[THE NEW OPPORTUNITY]
What I had discovered was [new opportunity name].
It's [mechanism in plain language — use "kinda like" bridge if needed].
That's why [result is possible for people who use this vehicle].
And that's what I'm going to share with you today.
```

**Storytelling mechanics:**
- Write at 3rd-grade reading level (shorter words, shorter sentences)
- Use the "kinda like" bridge for any concept past 3rd-grade level: "[Complex concept] — it's kinda like [familiar analogy]"
- Describe physical environments and bodily sensations — this is what makes readers *feel* the story
- Show the feeling, don't just report the event

## The Perfect Webinar / VSL Framework

Three-phase structure for any sales presentation, webinar, or long-form VSL.

### Phase 1: The Big Domino (Opening — 16% of total time/length)
- Hook: Pattern-disrupting statement or question that opens a loop around the Big Domino
- Rapport: "I was once where you are" — identification before authority
- Origin Story: Your Epiphany Bridge story introducing the new opportunity

### Phase 2: The Three Secrets (Content — 50% of total time/length)
Three story-based teaching sections, each attacking the Big Domino from a different angle.

**Each secret follows this structure:**
1. Framework name (named, memorable)
2. Epiphany Bridge story for this specific insight
3. Strategy (the *what* — never the *how*)
4. Social proof: case study or testimonial showing it working

- **Secret 1** → Breaks Vehicle false belief
- **Secret 2** → Breaks Internal (identity) false belief
- **Secret 3** → Breaks External (circumstance) false belief

**Critical rule:** Teach the *what*, not the *how*. The desire for the *how* is what they're buying. If you give away implementation, you remove the reason to buy.

**Transition check:** "If you did Secret 1, then Secret 2, then Secret 3 — could you get [the result]?" If the answer is yes, the Big Domino has fallen. Move to the offer.

### Phase 3: The Stack and Closes (Offer — 34% of total time/length)
See the Stack section below.

## The Stack: Offer Architecture

**Core insight:** Prospects remember the *last thing you showed them* and associate the price with that. Show everything simultaneously as a running total, so the last thing seen before the price is the full offer.

**Stack structure:**
For each component:
1. Introduce the component
2. Tell the brief story of how you created/earned/learned it
3. State its standalone value
4. Add it to the visible running total

Show the complete Stack before revealing the price. When price is revealed alongside everything it buys, the price feels anchored to total value rather than to the last item mentioned.

**Stack order by impact:**
1. Core offer (primary deliverable)
2. Frameworks / systems / tools
3. Done-for-you or done-with-you components
4. Quick-win component (delivers result in 24-72 hours)
5. Exclusive access (community, events, direct access)
6. Named bonuses with stated individual values

**Written copy version:**
```
Here's everything included in [product name]:

[Component Name] — [one-paragraph story of how this was created and why it matters]
Value: $XXX

[Component Name] — [story]
Value: $XXX

[Bonus Name] — [story]
Value: $XXX

Total value: $X,XXX
Your investment today: $XXX
```

## The Sixteen Closes (Quick Reference)

Use these in sequence during the Stack section. Not all 16 in every funnel — select based on the most likely remaining objections.

| Close | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|
| **Recap Close** | Before the offer — "If you did X, Y, Z — could you get [result]?" Confirms Big Domino fell. |
| **Question Close** | Transition to selling: "Can I show you how to implement this even faster?" |
| **If/All Close** | "If everything I showed you is true, what I'm about to share is the logical next step." |
| **Value Close** | Walk through each Stack item with story + individual value stated. |
| **Price Drop Close** | Total stacked value vs. actual price. "This alone is $X. Today the full package is $Y." |
| **Guarantee Close** | Specific, credible risk removal. Name what they get back and under what conditions. |
| **Bonus Urgency Close** | Time-limited bonus. Only works if genuine. |
| **I Owe You Close** | Address unspoken "why should I pay?" objection proactively. |
| **Works If You Work It** | Pre-handles "I won't follow through." Positions the product as serious and the buyer as capable. |
| **Conviction Close** | Express genuine belief with specific detail. Conviction ≠ hype. |
| **Two Kinds of People** | "There are two kinds of people who see this..." — creates identity-level choice. |
| **Future Close** | "Imagine 6 months from now, after you've implemented..." — future pacing creates felt desire. |
| **Cost of Doing Nothing** | Flip the risk: what does inaction cost per month in [time/money/opportunity]? |
| **Social Proof Close** | Transformation stories from people recognizably similar to the audience. |
| **Last Chance Close** | Real deadline, real scarcity only. Fake urgency destroys trust permanently. |
| **Recap + Invitation** | Final CTA: restate Stack, price, guarantee, one clear action. "Here's what you get. Here's what it costs. Here's my guarantee. Here's the link." |

## Headline Patterns by Context

**DTC Physical:**
- "Why [conventional approach] keeps failing you — and the [mechanism] that actually works"
- "The real reason [problem persists] has nothing to do with [thing they blame themselves for]"
- "New research shows [mechanism] — not [conventional belief] — is why [result] is hard to achieve"

**DTC Info Product:**
- "How [specific customer type] go from [current state] to [desired state] in [timeframe] using [named system]"
- "Why [typical customer] is stuck at [level] — and the one thing that changes everything"
- "The [named system] that [specific outcome]: why the old way of [doing X] is costing you [cost]"

**B2B AI/Consulting:**
- "[Specific role] at [specific company type]: here is what changes when [named system] joins your team"
- "Why [specific problem] is not an [ops/sales/marketing] problem — and the [category] that actually fixes it"
- "The [metric] gap between [winner companies] and everyone else is now [named capability]"

## Anti-Patterns Checklist

Before submitting any funnel copy, confirm none of these are present:

- [ ] Leading with features, ingredients, or product specs in the headline
- [ ] Improvement framing ("better," "faster," "more proven") in the headline
- [ ] "Clinically tested" or "proven" without explaining what was tested and found
- [ ] Social proof that is only star ratings with no story
- [ ] Transformation claims without specificity (numbers, timeframes, circumstance)
- [ ] Offer description that could apply to any competitor
- [ ] Stack reveals price before showing full value
- [ ] Three Secrets that teach the *how* rather than the *what*
- [ ] Missing one of the three false belief categories (vehicle / internal / external)
- [ ] Multiple CTAs competing for attention at the close
