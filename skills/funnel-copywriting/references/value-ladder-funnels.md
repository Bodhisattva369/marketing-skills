# Value Ladder and Funnel Type Selection

## The Value Ladder

Every sustainable offer ecosystem has multiple entry points. The ladder creates a deliberate progression from low-cost/low-commitment to high-cost/high-transformation.

```
CONTINUITY / HIGHEST TIER
  Premium membership, mastermind, retainer, ongoing services
  $2,000–$25,000+/year
       ↑
BACKEND
  High-touch, high-transformation
  Done-for-you, coaching, consulting, intensives
  $2,000–$25,000+
       ↑
CORE OFFER
  Primary product — what your brand is known for
  $97–$2,000
       ↑
FRONTEND / TRIPWIRE
  Entry-level paid product — low price, immediate value, quick win
  $7–$97
       ↑
BAIT
  Free or near-free — highest volume entry point
  Lead magnet, free webinar, free trial, book
  $0–$7
```

**The goal is not maximum conversion at any single rung.** The goal is moving customers up the ladder over time. The bait gives a taste of the transformation. Each rung delivers a deeper version of the same journey.

When the ladder is coherent — every offer the natural next step of the same story — conversion rates increase at every level because the customer always knows where they are going next.

## Funnel Type by Ladder Position

### Bait: Lead Generation Funnels
**Goal:** Capture email in exchange for immediate value

Best formats:
- **Lead magnet funnel** — free PDF, checklist, or guide for email opt-in
- **Free + shipping funnel** — physical book/guide at $0, customer pays shipping
- **Webinar registration** — free training for registration
- **Free trial** — product/software access, card captured for conversion
- **Quiz funnel** — diagnosis or assessment → email opt-in, results page

**Copy requirements:**
- Headline promises a specific, immediate outcome
- Friction must be near-zero — this is the first action, hardest to get
- Confirmation page and first email immediately begin the movement narrative
- The bait must over-deliver — a bad first experience poisons the entire ladder

### Frontend: Tripwire / Self-Liquidating Offer
**Goal:** Convert leads to buyers, cover ad spend, pre-qualify for core offer

Best formats:
- Low-priced product ($7–$47) + immediate OTO at checkout
- Flash sale / time-limited offer on a specific product

**Copy requirements:**
- Headline promises a specific quick win that delivers value immediately
- Price is low enough to be an impulse decision — no deliberation required
- The OTO at checkout is the natural next step toward the core offer:
  "You just bought [X]. If you want to get the result 3x faster, add [Y] right now for just $Z. This offer disappears when you leave this page."

### Core Offer: Sales Funnel (Long-Form)
**Goal:** Convert warm leads to the primary paid product

Best formats:
- **Webinar funnel** — registration → live or automated presentation → replay → close
- **Video Sales Letter (VSL)** — long-form video + order page
- **Long-form sales page** — written equivalent of the VSL
- **Demo/discovery call funnel** — for $500+ B2B offers

**Copy requirements:**
- Full Perfect Webinar / Three-Phase structure (Big Domino → Three Secrets → Stack)
- Complete Four Core Stories covering all three false belief categories
- Stack with cumulative value building before price reveal
- Guarantee that removes perceived risk of the decision
- Multiple CTAs for buyers who are ready before the end of the presentation

### Backend: High-Ticket / Application Funnels
**Goal:** Qualify and convert for high-touch, high-transformation offers

Best formats:
- **Application + phone sales call** — page with qualification form, call booking
- **Strategy session funnel** — free or low-cost call that converts to paid program
- **Challenge funnel** — 5–7 day free challenge that builds belief before backend offer

**Copy requirements:**
- Application page qualifies the prospect — copy should filter for ideal fit, not just volume
- Focus on transformation and exclusivity rather than features
- Social proof must be case-study depth, not testimonial snippets
- The close happens on the call, not on the page

### Continuity: Membership / Subscription Funnels
**Goal:** Convert one-time buyers to recurring revenue

Best formats:
- Monthly or annual subscription (community, content, coaching access)
- Retainer (B2B ongoing service)
- Box subscription (DTC physical product)

**Copy requirements:**
- Lead with the ongoing transformation, not the content library or feature set
- Address "can I cancel?" proactively — show what consistent access produces over 3–6–12 months
- Anchor the subscription price to the cost of the outcome it replaces
- Show community as the primary value accelerator (peer proof + accountability)

## Funnel Architecture by Context

### DTC Physical Products

```
BAIT:        Free quiz ("What's Your [Category] Type?") → segmented email opt-in
FRONTEND:    Starter kit ($27–$47) + OTO for 3-month supply at checkout
CORE:        Full supply bundle ($97–$197) + subscription prompt
BACKEND:     Coaching / accountability program ($297–$997/year)
CONTINUITY:  Auto-ship subscription with exclusive member pricing
```

Key notes:
- The quiz funnel is highly effective for segmentation and personalized follow-up
- A physical "book" or protocol guide as free + shipping creates buyers who are significantly more likely to convert to the supplement
- Subscription conversion happens at checkout with the core offer, not as a separate funnel step: "Subscribe and save 25%"
- The Stack for physical products must go beyond the product: include the system/protocol, guides, community access, and coaching before revealing the price

### DTC Info Products

```
BAIT:        Free masterclass / webinar (60–90 min) → email opt-in
FRONTEND:    Mini-course or workshop ($27–$97)
CORE:        Full program or course ($297–$997)
BACKEND:     Group coaching, mastermind, or intensive ($2,000–$10,000)
CONTINUITY:  Monthly community + live Q&A ($97–$297/month)
```

Key notes:
- The webinar funnel is the highest-converting warm-traffic funnel for offers $297+
- The challenge funnel (5-day free) is especially effective for building belief before a launch and creating community momentum
- Ascension must be explicitly communicated at each stage: "The next step for people who want to go deeper is..."
- Info product testimonials must be transformation stories with before/after specificity, not just star ratings

### B2B AI Tooling + Consulting

```
BAIT:        Free audit / ROI calculator / benchmark report → demo or call request
FRONTEND:    Diagnostic workshop ($500–$2,000, or free for qualified prospects)
CORE:        Initial implementation project ($5,000–$25,000)
BACKEND:     Ongoing optimization retainer ($2,000–$10,000/month)
CONTINUITY:  Platform licensing / SaaS subscription
```

Key notes:
- Primary conversion goal is a qualified discovery call — not a purchase
- The landing page functions as an application page: copy should qualify before booking
- "Free audit" or "ROI calculator" as bait is highly effective — delivers personalized value immediately and creates a natural conversation opener
- Case studies replace testimonials and must include specific metrics, timeline, company type/size, and before-state description
- The Stack in B2B is built around risk reduction, not value accumulation: what de-risks the decision?

## The OTO / Upsell Sequence

At each point of purchase, offer the natural next step immediately. The psychology: a person who has just said yes is in a yes-state and is maximally likely to say yes again.

OTO framing formula:
> "You've already decided to [what they just did]. The most common question we get at this point is 'what's the fastest way to get to [result] even faster?' That's exactly what [upsell name] does. For just $[price] more — available only right now on this page — you get [specific benefit]."

The OTO must be:
- The natural next step, not a random upsell
- Priced at a fraction of the core offer (impulse threshold, no deliberation)
- Genuinely valuable on its own, not a stripped-down teaser

A bad OTO experience at checkout trains the customer that your upsells are exploitative. A great OTO is the thing they think "oh I definitely want that too."
